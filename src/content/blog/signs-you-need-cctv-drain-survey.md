A CCTV drain survey is a non-invasive method of inspecting the inside of your drainage pipes using a small, waterproof camera. This technology allows drainage professionals to identify problems such as blockages, cracks, root intrusion, and structural damage without the need for disruptive excavation. Understanding when a CCTV survey might be beneficial can help you address drainage issues early, potentially saving time, money, and inconvenience in the long run.

One of the most common signs that you may need a CCTV drain survey is recurring blockages. If you find yourself regularly dealing with slow-draining sinks, toilets that do not flush properly, or drains that back up despite being cleared, there may be an underlying issue within your pipes that simple unblocking cannot resolve. A CCTV survey can reveal the root cause, whether it is a partial collapse, a build-up of debris, or damage that is allowing blockages to reform.

Unpleasant odours emanating from your drains are another indicator that something may be wrong. Foul smells can be caused by stagnant water, trapped waste, or even a break in the pipe that is allowing sewage to leak into the surrounding soil. If you notice persistent bad smells despite regular cleaning, a camera inspection can help pinpoint the source of the problem and guide the appropriate repair.

If you are purchasing a new property, a CCTV drain survey is highly recommended as part of your due diligence. Standard property surveys typically do not include an assessment of the drainage system, yet problems with drains can be costly to repair and may not be immediately apparent. A pre-purchase drain survey provides a clear picture of the condition of the pipes, helping you to make an informed decision and potentially negotiate on the sale price if issues are discovered.

Visible signs of subsidence or damp patches in your garden or around the foundations of your property can also suggest a drainage problem. Leaking or broken pipes beneath the ground can erode soil and cause water to pool in unexpected areas. Over time, this can lead to structural damage to your home. A CCTV survey can help to identify whether a faulty drain is contributing to these issues, allowing for targeted repairs.

If you notice that your drains are making unusual gurgling sounds when water is draining, this could indicate a partial blockage or a venting issue within the system. Gurgling occurs when air is trapped and then released as water flows through the pipes. While this might seem like a minor inconvenience, it can be a sign of a more significant obstruction or damage that warrants further investigation.

Properties with older drainage systems, particularly those built before modern plastic pipes became standard, may be more susceptible to problems such as cracks, displacement, and root ingress. If your home is more than a few decades old and you have never had the drains inspected, a CCTV survey can provide valuable insight into their current condition and help you plan for any necessary maintenance or repairs.

Finally, if you are planning building work or an extension that may affect the existing drainage layout, a CCTV survey is a sensible precaution. Understanding the precise location and condition of your drains before construction begins can help avoid accidental damage and ensure that any new connections are made correctly.

In summary, a CCTV drain survey is a cost-effective way to gain a detailed understanding of your drainage system. Whether you are dealing with persistent problems, buying a property, or simply want peace of mind, a professional inspection can provide the answers you need and help you take the right steps to protect your home.
